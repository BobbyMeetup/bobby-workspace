Losing access to your Edge account can be concerning, given its focus on privacy and security. Unlike traditional wallets or exchanges, Edge does not possess the ability to reset your password or retrieve your username. This guide provides users with clear, privacy-empowering steps to recover access, while highlighting Edge’s secure, self-custody approach and the tools available in critical scenarios.

# How Edge Handles Login Credentials

Edge never stores your username or password and cannot access or manage your credentials. Your login is protected via client-side encryption, putting only you in control. This privacy-first model ensures you have full custody—no one, not even Edge, can interfere with your access or recover information on your behalf.

# Recovering Your Username

-   Make a list of all possible usernames you may have created.
    
-   Try creating a new account with each username. If the username already exists, it might be yours.
    

# Resetting a Forgotten Password

-   Attempt to recall and enter possible passwords. Edge won’t lock your account for multiple tries, but you may be prompted to wait briefly after many attempts.
    
-   Search your email for 'Edge Recovery Token.' If you have a Recovery Token, follow the instructions to reset your password securely.
    
-   If PIN login is enabled on an authorized device, use your PIN. Logging in and out multiple times may trigger the password reset feature.
    

# Last Resort: Manual Recovery Options

-   If you know your username and nothing else works, you might be able to obtain your encrypted Login Package. Specialized recovery services may help brute-force this, but Edge cannot do this for you.
    

# Conclusion: Stay Secure & Proactive

Edge empowers you with control and privacy; only you have access to your login details. Store usernames, passwords, and backup tokens securely to avoid permanent loss. For ongoing security, consider enabling recovery methods and keeping devices authorized. Reach out to Edge support for guidance, but remember: your security starts with you.
