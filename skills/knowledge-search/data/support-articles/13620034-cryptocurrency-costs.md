Edge provides our users with a self-custodial solution to storing their cryptocurrency assets, which means that transactions always need to be broadcasted and processed by the network. Edge does not collect any fees or process any transactions ourselves, this is all handled by our exchange providers and the cryptocurrency networks independently.

  
*Costs to consider when making cryptocurrency transactions:*

**Network Fee (aka “gas”)**

All cryptocurrency transactions incur a small fee in order to be processed and confirmed by the blockchain network. This is the only fee that applies when simply sending funds to another address on the same network and goes directly to the miners/validators of the network. Transactions during times of high activity may see higher network fees due to the increased demand for limited block space.  
  
**Exchange Fee**

The exchange facilitating the swap will typically charge a percentage of the volume that is being transacted (ranging from 0.5%-3%+). This fee covers the costs incurred by the exchange, platform, and liquidity providers.

Important Note: Cross-chain exchanges (swapping across different networks) will nearly always be slower and more costly than swapping between assets on the same network. If you would like to minimize fees, then it is a good idea to consider swapping between wrapped assets and/or stable coins on the same network to avoid cross-chain charges.

**Slippage (exchange rate)**

Slippage occurs when the value of the currency received from an exchange is lower than the value of what was deposited. Some slippage is natural, as you can expect the exchange to charge for handling the swap order, but the total variance fluctuates significantly - particularly during times of high *volatility* and when there is low liquidity available between the two assets you are exchanging.  
  
*Always pay very close attention and review the funds received in the quote estimate BEFORE submitting any transaction - see example screenshot below of quote screen from an Edge exchange.*

  
**Other Costs**

-   Activation Fee:
    
-   -   A very small handful of cryptocurrency networks do require a minimum balance for the wallet to maintain active (ex. XRP, HBAR, XLM). This means that the first deposit into the address must be a minimum that is typically around $1-5 and that the minimum balance must remain for the life of the wallet. Please note, if anyone is telling you that you must first deposit funds to “activate” some account or in order to withdraw, then they are certainly attempting to scam you out of your coins.
        
-   Claim Bridged Funds:
    
-   -   Very occasionally, when using a third party bridging service such as Wormhole (https://wormhole.com/), a user may need to access the exchange using the destination network wallet and pay a small network fee for the bridged funds to be sent to your wallet address. This charge does not cost more than a single network transaction and such should never exceed ~$5. Again, scammers online are trying to steal your coins and may claim try to convince you to connect your wallet to a fraudulent website or send them money in order to receive money.
        
-   Withdrawal Fee:
    
-   -   Some “custodial” trading platforms may charge a *small* fee when withdrawing funds from their platform. This is intended to cover their own processing costs and will typically be a very small flat rate ($1 or less) but could be a percentage of the funds being withdrawn. This fee is paid by the funds being withdrawn, so proceed with **extreme** caution if there is any platform demanding you pay them in order to withdraw from their platform.
