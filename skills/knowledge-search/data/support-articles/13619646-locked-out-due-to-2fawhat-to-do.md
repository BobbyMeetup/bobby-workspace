Edge prioritizes security and privacy for all users, using two-factor authentication (2FA) to help protect your cryptocurrency assets. If you’re locked out of your Edge account because you no longer have access to your 2FA code or backup, don’t worry—there are built-in options for recovery. Follow this guide to safely and efficiently regain access to your account.

# Understanding Edge 2FA Lockout

Edge uses client-side encryption and a robust 2FA process to help prevent unauthorized access. If you lose access to your 2FA code or backup, your account is temporarily locked to ensure no one else can bypass your security.

# Steps to Regain Access

1.  Attempt login from a previously trusted device. If the device is permissioned, you may log in without the 2FA code.
    
2.  If you cannot access a trusted device, try logging in with correct username and password from an unrecognized device. Edge will prompt you to wait seven days before you can permission this new device.
    
3.  After the seven-day waiting period, return to Edge on the unrecognized device and proceed with the prompts to regain access.
    

# Best Practices After Regaining Access

-   Securely back up your 2FA seed or recovery codes (write them down and store in a safe location).
    
-   Set up recovery options, such as password reset reminders, within the Edge app.
    
-   Avoid sharing sensitive information with anyone, including recovery codes.
    

# Conclusion: Regaining Security and Access

Edge empowers you to maintain complete control over your crypto assets, even during a lockout. By following the above steps and best practices, you can quickly and securely reclaim your account. Always back up your 2FA and use all available Edge security features to safeguard your digital assets.
