**Why Was My Paybis Transaction Declined When Using Revolut?**

If your Paybis transaction was declined when using Revolut, it may be due to banking restrictions, verification issues, or internal Revolut security flags. Here’s a quick overview of possible causes and how to resolve them:

**Common Reasons**

1.  **Bank Restrictions:** Revolut may block crypto-related transactions depending on the jurisdiction or payment method used
    
2.  **Insufficient Funds:** Your linked Revolut currency account may not have enough balance, even if other accounts do
    
3.  **Verification Incomplete:** Either your Revolut or Paybis account may be missing KYC or ID verification
    
4.  **Incorrect Payment Info:** Card or transfer details may be outdated or incorrect
    
5.  **Security Review:** Revolut may flag the transaction as unusual or high-risk
    
6.  **Transaction Limits:** You may have hit Revolut’s or Paybis’s transfer or purchase limits
    

**Recommended Actions**

1.  Ensure your Revolut account has sufficient funds in the correct currency
    
2.  Double-check your card or payment method details in Revolut
    
3.  Complete all verification steps on both Revolut and Paybis
    
4.  Contact Revolut support to confirm they allow crypto purchases with your method
    
5.  Monitor emails and app notifications for any flags from Paybis or Revolut
    
6.  Retry with a different Revolut currency account or card if needed
    
7.  [**Contact Paybis Support**](https://paybis.com/contacts/)**: https://paybis.com/contacts/**
