Support requests can sometimes overlap or address the same concern. At Edge, efficiently merging and managing support requests ensures you get clear, consolidated answers while maintaining the security of your information. This article explains what happens when your request is merged with another, and how it streamlines your experience in the Edge ecosystem.

# What Does It Mean to Merge Support Requests?

Merging support requests means combining multiple related tickets into one. This often happens if you send multiple messages on a single issue, or if your question is similar to another unresolved case, ensuring all information is in one place for quicker, more coherent support.

# How the Merging Process Works at Edge

1.  A new request is reviewed and identified as related to an existing ticket.
    
2.  Support securely merges the new ticket into the original, combining all conversations and attachments.
    
3.  Further communication continues under the main ticket, maintaining your privacy and history in Edge’s zero-knowledge environment.
    

# Benefits of Merged Requests for Edge Users

-   Reduces confusion by centralizing updates and answers in one thread
    
-   Helps Edge support teams maintain privacy and accurate history
    
-   Speeds up resolution so you can get back to secure, empowered trading
    

# What to Expect After Your Request Is Merged

-   Communication continues only under the primary ticket number.
    
-   You’ll receive notifications for updates through that main request.
    
-   Your information remains protected under Edge’s privacy-first model.
    

# Conclusion: Confidence in Secure, Streamlined Support

Merging support tickets in Edge creates a seamless, privacy-centric support experience. By centralizing conversations, our team can resolve your concerns quickly and securely, empowering you to focus on managing your crypto, not tracking disparate support threads.
