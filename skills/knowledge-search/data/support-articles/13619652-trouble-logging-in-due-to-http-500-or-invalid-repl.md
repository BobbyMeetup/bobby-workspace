Edge prioritizes secure and private access, but occasionally technical issues can hamper your ability to log in. If you experience an HTTP 500 status code or see an 'Invalid reply JSON' error after entering your PIN, don't worry. There are alternative login methods you can use while PIN login is temporarily unavailable. Doing so can help you stay connected to your digital assets and manage your crypto wallet as usual.

# Understanding the Login Issue

Some users report encountering an HTTP 500 error or an 'Invalid reply JSON' message when using a PIN to log in. This usually indicates a temporary server-side or network communication issue impacting only the PIN authentication process, not your account's security or the Edge application's integrity.

# Recommended Alternative Login Methods

1.  Try Biometric Authentication: Use FaceID or fingerprint if your device supports these features and you have set them up in Edge.
    
2.  Sign in with Username and Password: Enter your full username and your Edge account password manually for secure access.
    

# Important Security Notes

-   Do not delete your Edge app unless you are absolutely certain you know both your username and password.
    
-   Edge cannot reset usernames or passwords for you—this is part of our commitment to privacy and user empowerment.
    
-   Safeguard your account credentials in a secure, private location.
    

# What to Do if You’re Still Locked Out

1.  Double-check your username and password for typos.
    
2.  Reference any secure backup or written copy of your credentials.
    
3.  Contact Edge Support at support@edge.app for guidance (note: account recovery is only possible if you have your credentials).
    

# Conclusion

Experiencing login errors like HTTP 500 or 'Invalid reply JSON' is frustrating but manageable. Use biometric or username/password login alternatives, and verify you have a secure copy of your credentials. Edge’s user-empowering privacy model ensures your account is secure—even during technical disruptions.
