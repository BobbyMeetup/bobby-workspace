# What this error means

Most of the time, **“Invalid API key” appears when your Edge app isn’t updated** to the current version or when your device's internet connection is having issues.

\*If you encounter this error please make sure your application is full updated and check to see if your device has strong internet connection.

---

# Update Edge on your device

## Android (Google Play)

1.  Open **Google Play Store**.
    
2.  Tap your **profile icon** → **Manage apps & device** → **Updates available**.
    
3.  Find **Edge** → tap **Update**.
    
    -   If you don’t see it, pull to refresh, or search **“Edge Wallet”** and update from the app page.
        

## iOS (App Store)

1.  Open **App Store**.
    
2.  Tap your **profile icon** (top right).
    
3.  Scroll to **Available Updates**.
    
4.  Find **Edge** → tap **Update**.
    
    -   If you don’t see it, pull down to refresh the list, or search **“Edge Wallet”** and update from the app page.
        

**\*After updating:** reopen Edge and retry the action that failed.

---

# Troubleshooting your internet connection

-   Toggle **Airplane mode** on/off, then retry.
    
-   Switch between **Wi-Fi and cellular**.
    
-   Temporarily disable **VPN/ad-blockers/private DNS** and retry.

# Still seeing “Invalid API key” after updating?

You may have hit a **bug** or a temporary **partner outage**. Please send us a report so we can debug quickly.

1.  **Take a screenshot** of the error as it appears.
    
2.  In Edge, tap **Help → Submit a support ticket Or Call for assistance**  
    Let us know:
    
    -   What you were doing (Buying, Selling, Swapping, etc)
        
    -   **Device & OS** (Tap help button to view version)
        
    -   Screen recordings or **screenshots are helpful**
        
        **\*We’ll do our best to diagnose the issue with you and coordinate with the partner if needed.**
